Welcome to the Peters Molecular Arbovirology Research Laboratory

We are a research group at The University of Queensland focused on advancing knowledge in molecular arbovirology and vector-borne diseases. Explore our research, meet our team, and discover our latest publications.

About Us

////////////////////////////

TRANSLATING INNOVATIVE RESEARCH INTO POWERFUL DISEASE CONTROL STRATEGIES

The Peters Lab is based at The School of Chemistry and Molecular Biosciences, UQ. Our mission is to advance the understanding of mosquito-borne viruses and their vectors through innovative research, collaboration, and training the next generation of scientists.

////////////////////////////

RESEARCH AREAS

Arbovirus Discovery

Identifying and characterizing novel arboviruses to expand our understanding of emerging infectious diseases.

Our research uses cutting-edge metagenomics and next-generation sequencing technologies to discover previously unknown arboviruses in mosquito populations, enabling rapid identification of potential emerging threats to human and animal health.

Diagnostics

We develop rapid and accurate diagnostic assays for arboviral infections using serological approaches.

Our diagnostic platforms enable early detection of infections, facilitating timely clinical intervention and epidemiological surveillance. We focus on point-of-care tests that can be deployed in resource-limited settings.

Vaccines

Our vaccine research focuses on developing safe and effective immunizations against mosquito-borne viruses.

Through innovative approaches including live-attenuated and reverse genetics strategies, we work to prevent arboviral diseases and protect public health globally.

RESEARCH PLATFORM

////////////////////////////

ISVAC PLATFORM

The Insect-Specific Virus (ISV) vaccine platform represents a groundbreaking approach to viral vaccine development. This platform leverages insect-specific viruses as vectors to safely deliver protective antigens, offering a new paradigm for vaccine engineering.

Our research demonstrates the potential of ISVac as a biological control agent for mosquito-borne diseases, providing both therapeutic and environmental benefits.

OUR REAGENTS

////////////////////////////

MOZZY MABS

As an integral part of our investigations we have produced monoclonal antibodies (mAbs) to many different viruses. These include an extensive panel of antibodies to mosquito-borne pathogens (eg. Zika, West Nile and Chikungunya viruses) as well as unique sets of mAbs to several commensal viruses of mosquitoes (insect-specific viruses).

These antibodies have been used as research tools, as diagnostic and immunotherapeutic agents or for reference and quality-control purposes. Because of their broad utility, we receive frequent requests for these reagents from research or industry groups around the world. We have developed Mozzy Mabs to fulfill these requests sustainably and to increase accessibility to these reagents by the scientific community.
